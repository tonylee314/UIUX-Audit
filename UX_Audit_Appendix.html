Limitations

This review covers a small pool of 6 competitors and is not intended to represent the full landscape of AI red teaming products. Analysis was also constrained to publicly available UIs, which may not reflect the complete functionality of each tool. These factors limit the generalizability of the findings.

RQ1 — Data Visualization

Competitors prioritize data richness over delivery.

Across all 6 competitors, the common approach is to show everything — resulting in a UI that overwhelms rather than informing users. This makes it difficult for users to understand the vulnerability status of their models and what to look at first.

Critical
📊
Overly complex diagram types
Diagrams that demand interpretation instead of delivering insight fail their users.
5 of 6 found Sankey diagrams unreadable
High
❓
Unclear scoring logic
Users who don't understand a score can't act on it.
Cited in 2 of 6 competitors
High
📋
Visual and cognitive overload
Dense layouts force users to work to find insight — rather than surfacing it.
Cited in 2 of 6 competitors

"I probably spend as much time trying to understand what they're trying to tell me as I would doing it myself."

— Workshop Participant
Core insight

Effective data visualization isn't about showing all the findings — it's about helping users understand the status of their models at one glance and the risks clearly.

RQ2 — Actionability

Users are left to connect the dots themselves.

Even when findings are surfaced, the path from "what was found" to "what do I do next" is unclear. Users are unsure what vulnerability needs to be prioritized, and how a vulnerability impacts the business.

High
📝
Recommendations restate findings
Users need specific next steps, not a restatement of the problem.
Observed in 2 of 6 competitors
Moderate
⚖️

Critical
🏢
Missing business context
The "why should I care" layer is almost entirely absent across competitors.
Gap identified across 5 of 6 competitors

"Understanding why I should care about the results is the key missing component from almost everyone. I'd have no idea how to interpret results."

Core insight

Nobody is effectively closing the loop. Typically, the assessment becomes a dead-end rather than a starting point for fixing issues.

Competitor Standouts

UX features worth flagging.

Despite broad gaps, some specific features across competitors were recognized for improving overall UX.

Feature type: Graph Status / Filter Action Navigation
Data Visualization
Model Performance MindGard Line History Graph
Gives users a temporal view of model performance over time — useful for reporting purposes.
Vulnerabilities by Intent Category Calypso Pie Graph
Simple, familiar chart type that lets users instantly see where vulnerability volume concentrates.
Attack Status (Success / Attempt / No Risk) SPLX Color-coded Tags
Three-state taxonomy with clear traffic light color coding makes result prioritization intuitive — users can filter by status type to reduce cognitive load.
Dashboard SPLX Status Bar
A single progress indicator that communicates model scan state embedded in the dashboard — reducing the need to dig through for basic status information.
Actionability
Apply Mitigation · View Logs Promptfoo Inline Action Buttons
Placing actions directly next to findings removes the friction of navigating elsewhere to act. The finding and the fix live together.
Sidebar Configuration Promptfoo PRISMA Step-by-step Flow
Guided onboarding improves users' mental model and reduces the setup burden — especially valuable for non-technical users who need scaffolding to get started.
Selected Findings MindGard Calypso Expandable Side Panels
Expandable actions help keep the main view uncluttered while giving users depth on demand — a strong pattern for balancing density and clarity.
Critical Summary Calypso Fixed Card Header
Keeping the critical summary context visible while scrolling through details helps users maintain orientation.
